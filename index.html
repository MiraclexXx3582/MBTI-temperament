Temperament refers to the biologically rooted part of an individual's personality. It encompasses the innate traits, natural predispositions, and characteristic patterns of reaction that define a person's fundamental nature. Unlike character, which is shaped by upbringing and moral choices, or personality, which is the complex result of social experience, temperament is considered the "foundation" of the psyche. It determines the general tempo, intensity, and energy of behavior—such as how quickly a person reacts, their level of emotional sensitivity, and their capacity for endurance. It is present from birth and remains relatively stable throughout a lifetime.
